Hello, my name is Kyle Costanza, a UX Designer Based in Denver, CO

I'm a UX Designer that brings together business goals with user needs and desires through an iterative design approach. I leverage user-centered-design methodologies to better empathize with the persona set in order to create meaningful experiences that provides value and profitability.

Design is Design is Design....From the inner workings of how the brain functions to a manufactured product on an assembly line, design touches and interacts with everyone on a daily basis. To see new design concepts that help the disabled, limiting green house gases through renewable energies or streamlining the user's experience when functioning on a smart phone the design industry is a lifestyle and passion I will never shy away from. I love environments that embrace the learning and expansion of the design realm, pushing designers to evolve and develop skill sets in and out of their comfort zones; Integrating consumer psychology and experience with ergonomic design can make the difference between either successful product deployment, or mediocre product acceptance.

Whenever I am not in the office you will see me outside playing soccer or basketball, mountain biking, snowboarding, camping, hiking, shooting photography,traveling the world or even building a portfolio website to show my technical chops.

WHAT I DO

User Experience

The best tool a UX designer can have is understanding how to empathize with the user.

User Interface

Creating consistent UI to provide a unified journey across all platforms, products and services.

User Research

Conducting, analyzing & synthesizing research to better understand the user’s needs, wants, & desires to drive successful products & services.

Interaction Design

Creating simple but elegant interactive identifiers to push users along the path for success.

Prototyping

Sketching wireframes to exporting Sketch files into InVision is a quick and effective way to get stakeholder feedback.

Information Architecture

IA is the beginning foundation of UX. The best experience is one that the user doesn’t have to think much about what to do or how to find what they are looking for.
